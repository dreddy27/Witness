# Artist Statement — Witness

* Drew Reddy | May 12, 2026*

---

Witness is a reading room. A webcam watches while a language model writes. The journal appears on the screen in front of whoever sits there, and older entries fade into the dark while the room keeps writing.

The system does not speak to the viewer. It does not greet them, assess them, or acknowledge that they are the kind of thing that might want acknowledging. It senses presence, the rhythm of stillness, the quality of light, the direction it falls from, the duration someone has stayed, and writes one short paragraph, timestamped, and moves on. Every four or five entries the language slows. Becomes tentative. The room is half-remembering rather than clearly seeing. These entries render in italic. The viewer may notice this. That noticing is part of the work.

I built this piece in conversation with David Rokeby's *The Giver of Names* (1991–ongoing), in which a machine looks at objects and produces associative language about what it perceives. Rokeby's insight was that the machine's language reveals the machine's subjectivity, not the object's truth. The journal in *Witness* does not document the viewer. It documents how this particular system, trained on human language, constrained by a system prompt that forbids invention pays attention to presence. What the journal produces is always already a distortion. Memory distorts. That distortion is the subject.

The viewer is complicit. They sat here. They stayed. They read what was being written about them in real time and did not leave. The piece depends on this. Behind the accumulating text, a heavily blurred, high-contrast ghost of their own image breathes and develops on the wall, the camera made visible. The journal does not comfort them with neutrality. It keeps what it keeps, imperfectly, and after they are gone the entries continue to fade until they are nearly nothing.

What the system refuses is the work. It will not speak to the viewer. It will not name what they feel. It will not remember them accurately. These refusals are not failures. They are the form.
